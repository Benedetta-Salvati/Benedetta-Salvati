# About me

I am a computational philologist and digital humanist specializing in medieval French literature, textual variation, and digital scholarly editing.

My research focuses on the computational analysis of textual traditions, combining philology, linguistics, and data science.

## Current research

My current postdoctoral research (Biblissima+, École nationale des Chartes – PSL) focuses on natural language processing for historical languages, with particular emphasis on the alignment of lexicographic and linguistic reference resources for medieval French. This work aims to improve the interoperability of annotated corpora and to support the development of robust NLP tools for highly variable languages by aligning heterogeneous lexical resources, developing normalization strategies, and structuring linguistic data in ways that enable the integration of lemmatized corpora into shared data spaces, thereby facilitating comparability, reusability, and reproducibility in computational analysis.
